**Short answers to your three questions:**

**Most accessible buyer persona:** Specialty Insurance (MGAs). ~200 buyers control the market — ABM at its cleanest. Well-defined title, high WTP, surgical conference and LinkedIn targeting. Mental health is #2 for inbound pull (clinician burnout narrative is strong SEO and content territory). Government is last — treat that as a BD motion, not a marketing motion, and don't allocate marketing budget there until FedRAMP is in hand.

**Strongest narrative differentiation:** Mental health wins this one. "Not a chatbot — a clinician multiplier" is genuine counter-positioning against every VC-funded patient-facing AI in that space. Legal SMB is close second — "BigLaw gets Harvey, you get nothing" is an honest narrative that bar associations will endorse. Both have positioning white space no well-capitalized player currently owns.

**Distribution thesis weaknesses:**
- **Legal**: Over-reliant on Clio/MyCase. Platform risk is high — both have active AI roadmaps and will absorb this functionality. Reframe: state bar PMAP endorsement as primary channel, platforms as technical connectors only.
- **Mental Health**: Targeting telehealth scale-ups (Headway, Alma) is targeting companies that will build in-house within 2 heartbeats. Reframe: employer EAP networks and health systems — neither will build internally.
- **Construction**: Direct SaaS with implementation support at this price point will destroy unit economics. Reframe: Procore App Marketplace as primary distribution. Lower CAC, lower implementation drag, immediate credibility.

One sequencing suggestion: consider moving mental health ahead of manufacturing in the entry queue from a marketing standpoint. Narrative is sharper and inbound pull is faster. Manufacturing is the larger TAM but the persona is more fragmented by sub-industry, which increases CPL.
